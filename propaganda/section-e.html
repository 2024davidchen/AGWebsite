Section E

Section E is mainly focused on choosing examples to strengthen the speaker's arguement

Techniques:

  1. Concurrency
  2. Post Hoc
  3. Selected Instances
  4. Hasty Generalization
  5. Faulty Analogy
  6. Composition
  7. Division
  8. Non Sequitor

Definitions:

  1. Concurrency

    The speaker states that because one thing happened during something else, one must have caused the other

    Simplified: Because A happened during B, B must have caused A

    Examples:

    • Biden was president when all the gas prices went up. It must be his fault that gas is so expensive.
  2. Post Hoc

    The speaker states because one thing happened after another, one must have caused the other

    Simplified: Because A happened after B, B must have caused A

    Examples:

    • Ever since Tara helped me get that hamster at the arcade, I've been getting a lot of kills in Overwatch. We should go to the arcade again and get something else so that I can do even better.
  3. Selected Instances

    The speaker chooses a few niche (rare) cases and uses them as if all cases are like this

    Examples:

    • I don't need to study Prop. David and Andrew didn't study and they did terrific.
  4. Hasty Generalization

    The speaker sees one thing happen and assumes that the future will be similar

    • Coach David tried to teach us Ling last week, but I didn't understand a thing. He must not be a very good coach.

    The speaker knows that two things are similar in one way, so they assume they are in another

  6. Composition

    These things are good individually, therefore they are good together

    Examples:

    • Mr. Torgue: "I probably shoulda set you up with a sponsor beforehand but I was busy suplexing a shark wearing a bolo tie when I should have been setting up sponsors." (Quote taken from Borderlands 2)
  7. Division

    This group is good, so if I take a part of it it must be good too

    Examples:

    • Mother to son: "I know you have a math test tomorrow, but I don't want to see you staying up too late."
  8. Non Sequitor

    Note: This means it is not 0 No Technique, make sure to look if there is actually propaganda (like if they are actually trying to persuade you of something)

    Usually you shouldn't think too hard about it, if it is a technique from another section they will usually make it very blatantly obvious

    Examples:

    • I don't understand why you don't like August. He's an angel; he doesn't swear and he's always really wholesome. (B2 Metaphor and Simile)

    HIGH SCHOOL ONLY: Non Sequitor instead means that the speaker uses logic incorrectly. If they are using the logic correctly, it is 0 No Technique

    Note: You have to actually use the logic and see if it applies, even if it isn't right if you think about it enough.

    1. Law of Detachment:

      If A happens then B happens. A happened. Therefore, B happened.

    2. Law of Contrapositive:

      If A happens then B happens. B did not happen. Therefore, A did not happen.

    3. Disjunctive Syllogism:

      Only A or B can happen. A didn't happen. Therefore B happened (Or if B didn't happen, A happened)

    4. Hypothetical Syllogism:

      A leads to B. B leads to C. Therefore, A leads to C

    • Mrs. Dean told us we'd do well on the test if we studied. Andy got an A+, so he must have studied. (8 Non Sequitor)
    • Mrs. Dean said I'll do well on the AP Exam if I studied. I studied last night, therefore I should do well. (0 No Technique)
    • August said he'd rank up to Silver 1 if he won that game yesterday. He got to Silver 1 so he must have won that game. (8 Non Sequitor)
    • Yuxuan said she'd finish my Amazon order if I did well on the practice ACT I did last night. She didn't finish my order so I must not have done well. (0 No Technique)
    • Mason said he'd either do well in Prop or Theme. He got 1st place in Theme, so he must not have done well in Prop. (8 Non Sequitor)
    • August said he'd either be asleep or talking on Discord when he woke up. He isn't online in Discord so he must still be asleep. (0 No Technique)
    • If I have extra time tonight I can study for the math test tomorrow. If I study for the math test tomorrow I'll do well. I don't have any extra time tonight so I won't do well on the test. (8 Non Sequitor)
    • If I get a job, I'll start making money. If I make money, I can buy whatever I want. I need to get a job to buy whatever I want. (0 No Technique)
